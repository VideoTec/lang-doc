https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uniform_Resource_Identifier

In information technology, 
a Uniform Resource Identifier (URI) is a string of characters used to identify a resource. 
Such identification enables interaction with representations of the resource over a network, 
typically the World Wide Web, using specific protocols. 
Schemes specifying a concrete syntax and associated protocols define each URI. 
The most common form of URI is the Uniform Resource Locator (URL), 
frequently referred to informally as a web address. 
More rarely seen in usage is the Uniform Resource Name (URN), 
which was designed to complement URLs 
by providing a mechanism for the identification of resources in particular namespaces.
